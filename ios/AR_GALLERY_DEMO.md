# AR Gallery Demo Guide

## Quick Demo Script

Want to show off the AR Gallery feature? Follow this quick demo script:

### Setup (30 seconds)
1. Open the ColoringBook app on an iOS device
2. Ensure you have at least one completed coloring page in your gallery
3. Make sure you're in a well-lit room with visible walls or flat surfaces

### Demo Flow (2 minutes)

#### Part 1: Accessing AR Gallery
1. **Show the dashboard**: "Here's my gallery of coloring pages"
2. **Tap a completed image**: "Let's view this one in detail"
3. **Point out the new button**: "Notice the new 'View in AR' button"
4. **Tap it**: "Let's see this in augmented reality!"

#### Part 2: Surface Detection
1. **Move the device slowly**: "The app is scanning the environment..."
2. **Wait for the message**: "See? It detected the wall/floor!"
3. **Explain**: "The app uses ARKit to understand the 3D space around us"

#### Part 3: Placing Artwork
1. **Point at a wall**: "Now I'll place my coloring page on this wall"
2. **Tap the wall**: "Just tap where I want it..."
3. **Show the result**: "And there it is! A virtual poster of my artwork!"

#### Part 4: Interactions
1. **Pinch to resize**: "I can make it bigger or smaller with a pinch gesture"
2. **Use size buttons**: "Or use these +/- buttons for precise control"
3. **Drag to move**: "I can drag it to a different spot"
4. **Show rotation**: "Walk around it - it stays on the wall!"

#### Part 5: Multiple Images (Optional)
1. **Reset the scene**: "Let me clear this and try something fun"
2. **Place 2-3 images**: "I can create a whole gallery wall!"
3. **Arrange them**: "Resize and position each one..."
4. **Show the result**: "Look - my own AR art gallery!"

#### Part 6: Screenshot
1. **Frame the shot**: "Let me capture this moment"
2. **Tap camera button**: "Take a screenshot..."
3. **Show confirmation**: "Saved to Photos! Now I can share it."

### Demo Tips

#### Lighting
- Use rooms with good lighting
- Avoid very dark spaces
- Indirect natural light works best

#### Surfaces
- Flat walls work great
- Tables and floors are perfect
- Avoid mirrors or very shiny surfaces
- Patterned surfaces detect better than plain white

#### Movement
- Move device slowly and smoothly
- Keep movements steady for best tracking
- Let AR "see" the surface from different angles

#### Positioning
- Start with medium-sized placement (30cm)
- Stand 1-2 meters from the wall
- Try different heights and arrangements

### Common Demo Questions & Answers

**Q: Does this work on all phones?**
A: Works on iPhone 6s and newer, all iPad Pros, and iPad 5th gen+

**Q: Do you need internet?**
A: Only to load the image initially. Once loaded, AR works offline.

**Q: Can you save the AR scene?**
A: You can take screenshots, but the 3D placement isn't saved (yet!)

**Q: How accurate is the placement?**
A: Very accurate! ARKit tracks with millimeter precision.

**Q: Can others see the art?**
A: No, it's only on your screen. But you can share screenshots!

**Q: Does it drain battery?**
A: AR uses more battery than normal, but it's optimized for efficiency.
